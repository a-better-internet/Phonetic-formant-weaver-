1 · What it is

The PFW-11 turns writing into sound. It ignores what words mean and instead treats every letter, number and mark of punctuation as a control signal for a synthesizer. You type or paste text; the machine scans it on a loop and “performs” it as drones, glides and percussion.

2 · Quick start

  1. Click ENGINE once — browsers require one click before they will make sound.
  2. Type letters into the command terminal, or press LOAD DEMO TEXT, or hit a preset like DEEP DRONE.
  3. Set Master to taste and watch the scope, the register and the trigger LEDs respond.

3 · How text becomes sound

Each character is routed to a different synthesis engine by phonetic category:

TypeKeysSound
VowelsA E I O U YSustained formant drones — the “voice.” Hold a vowel key for a continuous drone.
PlosivesP T K B D GPercussive strikes and plucks.
FricativesS Z F V HNoise textures, hiss, static.
Nasals / liquidsL R M NWarble and modulation.

Digraphs: letter pairs are caught first — gliding vowels (AY OY OW EY EA OA AU OO EE) slide smoothly from one vowel to the next, and consonant pairs (SH TH CH NG CK PH WH) get their own character.

Numbers & punctuation: digits 0–9 are a drum kit (0 = deep kick … 9 = crash); punctuation is accents and ticks (! and ? loud, . and , soft). Spaces and line breaks are rests, so the rhythm follows the shape of your text.

Case: UPPERCASE letters shout (louder and grittier); lowercase whispers (soft and pure). Write in mixed case to shape the phrasing.

4 · The scan (sequencer)

The entire text loops as an evolving pattern. The 16-cell register is a moving window onto that loop; STEP shows your position in the full buffer. Clock BPM sets the speed, Swing adds groove, and Vary introduces octave jumps, ghost rests and ratchets so no two passes are identical. Press Esc to lock the buffer — existing drones keep playing while you tweak knobs without spawning new layers.

8 · Try this

  • Paste a poem, hit CHOIR, and turn COARTIC on — the vowels pour into each other.
  • Type a beat with numbers (0 6 8 6) and cycle KIT while it loops.
  • Write in MiXeD case — capitals punch, lowercase softens.
  • Loop a plosive line and slowly sweep Grit from 0 to 100.
  • Hold a vowel, then raise Feedback for industrial self-oscillation.
  • Capture a take with ● REC (open the file standalone if recording is blocked in preview).
